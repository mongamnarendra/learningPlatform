Frequently Asked Questions

UI (User Interface) focuses on the look and feel of a product, including its visual elements and interactivity, while UX (User Experience) deals with the overall experience users have when interacting with a product, ensuring it's easy to use and meets their needs.

While coding is not mandatory for UI/UX designers, having a basic understanding of HTML, CSS, and JavaScript can be helpful for communicating with developers and understanding design limitations.

Popular tools include Adobe XD, Figma, Sketch, and InVision for design and prototyping, along with tools like Miro or Whimsical for wireframing and collaborative work.

This UI/UX design course is open to anyone interested in creating intuitive and engaging digital experiences.

The course is self-paced and can be completed in approximately 12 weeks with consistent effort.
