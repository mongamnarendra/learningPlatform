Frequently Asked Questions

Many cybersecurity courses are designed for beginners and don’t require prior experience. You’ll start with foundational topics like basic networking, security principles, and gradually move into more advanced topics like threat detection and ethical hacking.

In a cybersecurity course, you'll learn how to protect systems, networks, and data from cyber threats. Key skills include ethical hacking, network security, encryption, incident response, risk management, and learning how to identify and defend against different types of cyberattacks.

You’ll learn about various types of cyber threats, including phishing attacks, malware, ransomware, denial of service (DoS), SQL injection, and social engineering. The course will teach you how to prevent and respond to these threats in real-world scenarios.

Anyone with an interest in technology and a desire to protect digital systems can enroll in a cybersecurity course! This course is ideal for:Beginners, Students, Graduates

The course is self-paced and can be completed in approximately 12 weeks with consistent effort.
